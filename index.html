Most teenagers break the law. Teens of color are more likely to be punished for it. Scholars say this disparity stems from bias in police and courts.

Contact with the justice system puts teens at a disadvantage that persists beyond sentencing. It matters because, in the current system, the teens affected are disproportionately black.

By way of example, meet two groups of teen boys. Group A is black. Group B is white. (n = 3250)

In Chicago, black youth are overrepresented among juvenile arrests.

As a consequence, they represent a wide majority at every subsequent stage of the justice system.

Youth who are arrested are more likely than their peers to drop out of high school.

Students who are arrested are twice as likely than their peers to drop out of high school, and half as likely to attend a four-year college.
