Here are two groups of teen boys. (n = 3250)


Twenty percent of boys from each group use marijuana. (n = 650)


Fifteen boys from Group A are arrested for minor marijuana offenses.

Only one is arrested from Group B.

The difference?

Group A is black.

A broad body of research indicates that most juveniles will engage in "delinquent" behavior during their adolescence.

Youth of color are more likely to have an encounter with the criminal justice system. This phenomenon is known as disproportionate minority contact.

Black youth account account for 79 percent of juvenile arrests in Chicago. As a consequence, they represent a wide majority at every subsequent stage of the justice system.
