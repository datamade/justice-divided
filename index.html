A broad body of research indicates that most juveniles engage in “delinquent” behavior during their adolescence.

Youth of color are more likely to encounter the criminal justice system.

This phenomenon is known as “disproportionate minority contact.”

In Chicago, black youth account for 40 percent of the population between the ages of 10 and 17 – but 79 percent of juvenile arrests.
